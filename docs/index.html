EU-India Trade: FTA & CBAM Resource Hub

Two policy shifts are reshaping India-Europe trade at once: the EU-India Free Trade Agreement and the EU Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism. This hub collects practical, sourced guidance for exporters and procurement teams working the corridor.

The EU-India Free Trade Agreement

The EU-India FTA was concluded on 27 January 2026 and is currently in ratification. When it enters force it will cut tariffs on a large share of goods traded between India and the European Union. The detail that matters for planning sits in the tariff schedules, organised by HS code and staging category.

CBAM: the EU Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism

CBAM has been live since 1 January 2026. It puts a carbon price on imports of carbon-intensive goods entering the EU: iron and steel, aluminium, cement, fertilisers, hydrogen, and electricity. For exporters in these categories, the carbon embedded in a product now carries a cost at the EU border, and the lower tariffs coming under the FTA do not cancel it out. The two instruments run side by side.

Why both matter together

Modelling only the FTA tariff relief gives a landed-cost number that is too optimistic for carbon-heavy goods like steel and aluminium, and roughly right for low-carbon goods. The accurate approach is to model the FTA tariff and the CBAM cost together, by HS code. The exporters who win in the EU market over the next few years will be the ones who can show a verified, low carbon number alongside a competitive price.
